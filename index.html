Essential Brewing Principles

  • Use freshly ground coffee for best flavor.
  • Measure coffee by weight (scale) rather than volume for precision.
  • The right water temperature is between 90–96°C (195–205°F).
  • Adjust grind size for different brewing methods.


Common Brewing Methods

Pour Over

  • Flavor profile: Clean, highlights subtle flavors, allows for fine-tuning.
  • Grind: Medium-fine (table salt texture).
  • How: Place filter and grounds in a dripper (e.g., V60, Kalita Wave) over your cup or carafe. Slowly pour hot water in circles until the grounds are saturated, letting it drain through.
  • Ratio: Start with 1:16 coffee-to-water (e.g., 20g coffee to 320g water).

French Press

  • Flavor profile: Full-bodied, robust, sometimes slightly gritty.
  • Grind: Coarse (sea salt texture)
  • How: Add coffee and hot water to the carafe, stir, place plunger assembly on top, and steep 4–5 minutes. Press down slowly and pour.
  • Ratio: 1:15 coffee-to-water (e.g., 30g coffee to 450g water).


Espresso

  • Flavor profile: Very strong, concentrated, with crema.
  • Grind: Very fine (almost powder).
  • How: Use an espresso machine to force hot water under pressure through finely-ground coffee for 25–30 seconds.
  • Ratio: Typically 1:2 (18g coffee yields 36g espresso shot).

Cold Brew

  • Flavor profile: Smooth, low acidity, served cold.
  • Grind: Coarse.
  • How: Steep grounds in cold water for 12–18 hours, strain, and serve over ice or diluted with water or milk.
  • Ratio: 1:8 (e.g., 60g coffee to 480g water).
